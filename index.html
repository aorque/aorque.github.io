The Starting Point

When I joined F1 Arcade Las Vegas, the events function was essentially nonexistent. Two days of shadowing a manager in Boston. A brand guide. A few templates. That was it. No vendor relationships, no team comms infrastructure, no upsell tools, no playbooks, no events staff — just me.

What I Built

  • Full vendor repository from scratch — personally outreached to and onboarded ~15 vendors, all drawn from pre-existing relationships I'd built at Tag Group
  • All team communications infrastructure — stood up every operational group chat: events, AV, ops, kitchen, and logistics (Teams + iMessage)
  • Triple Seat system buildout — created custom templates and workflows that went significantly beyond the national standard, tailored to how Las Vegas operates
  • Complete upsell pipeline — built every upsell tool from scratch using Canva and Adobe Illustrator (personal account for the first 5 months): menus, floor plans, add-on packages, F&B packages, AV options, branded experiences
  • Email and handoff playbooks — built all client onboarding templates for the sales-to-events handoff, including the pre-event planning and upsell sequence I owned
  • Trained the ops team — solo for 2.5 months before getting support; eventually trained the full ops staff to execute events alongside me

The Numbers

  • Events consistently drove ~53% of total venue revenue within 5–6 months of operation
  • Estimated $3.3M total events revenue generated across the active period
  • $270K+ personally attributed to upsells I built and executed the pipeline for
  • Largest single client: PWC at $177K — the largest single-account expansion in my time there

Hired My Own Replacement

By February, the system was solid enough that I brought on a junior events manager (Laxia) whose primary role was client comms and outreach — because I'd already built everything else. She stepped into a functioning machine.

The Program

After Metafy's Series A ($26M raised), CEO created a $1M pledge to the grassroots gaming and esports community — micro-grants and sponsorships to local tournaments, content creators, and community-run events that major brands typically ignore. I was the program manager.

What I Owned

  • Full intake and evaluation system — built on Coda + Zapier + Google Workspace + Typeform, with cross-functional coordination across product, legal, and accounting
  • Every funding decision — evaluated and approved all applications personally across the life of the program
  • Refined the process continuously — started with an intern, went solo after month 2, and kept the pipeline running while also executing live IRL activations
  • On-site activation operations — staffing, scheduling, logistics, and vendor coordination at live grassroots events

Scale

  • Funded 400+ grassroots events over ~8–9 months
  • Sponsorships ranged from small community grants ($100 comedy show) to larger tournament sponsorships
  • Program built from scratch — no predecessor, no playbook
